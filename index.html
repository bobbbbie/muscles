Legs

The leg muscles are the largest muscle group in the body and include the quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves. Leg exercises are crucial for overall strength, stability, and athletic performance. Some exercises to train the leg muscles include squats, lunges, deadlifts, leg presses, and calf raises.

Back

The back muscles provide support, stability, and help maintain proper posture. They include muscles such as the latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, and erector spinae. To train the back muscles, you can perform exercises like pull-ups, rows (barbell rows, dumbbell rows), lat pulldowns, and back extensions.

Chest

The chest muscles, specifically the pectoralis major and pectoralis minor, are responsible for movements like pushing and add to the upper body strength. Exercises that target the chest muscles include bench press (barbell or dumbbell), push-ups, chest flyes, and cable crossovers.

Arms

The arm muscles consist of the biceps and triceps. The biceps are located on the front of the upper arm, while the triceps are located on the back of the upper arm. To train the biceps, exercises like bicep curls (dumbbell or barbell), hammer curls, and chin-ups are effective. For the triceps, exercises such as tricep dips, tricep pushdowns, and overhead tricep extensions can be performed..

Abs

The abdominal muscles, including the rectus abdominis (six-pack muscles) and obliques, contribute to core strength and stability. Exercises that target the abs include crunches, planks, Russian twists, leg raises, and bicycle crunches..
